# AI & Law Positioning of LEXON

LEXON is positioned within the AI & Law tradition of executable legal knowledge representation and rule-based normative reasoning.

## Relation to AI & Law

LEXON connects to:

- formal models of legal reasoning;
- statutory rule formalisation;
- legal expert systems;
- legal ontologies;
- normative reasoning systems;
- computational models of legal texts;
- evaluation and auditing techniques for legal AI systems.

## Contributions

LEXON contributes:

1. A typed schema for AI regulatory obligations.
2. A stratified rule layer for obligation activation and evidence-gap inference.
3. Three-valued applicability semantics for incomplete system profiles.
4. Actor-scoped obligation matching.
5. Direct conflict detection under an explicit incompatibility vocabulary.
6. Explicit separation between evidentiary completeness and substantive legal compliance.
7. Reproducible conformance tests and derivation traces.

## Non-claims

LEXON does not:

- provide legal advice;
- validate legal interpretations;
- decide substantive compliance;
- replace qualified legal or compliance review;
- claim external legal validity from the synthetic conformance suite.

## Intended role

LEXON is a machine-checkable representation and reasoning substrate for human-supervised AI regulatory review.
